\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{HEXA-FUSION}, a closed-form 7-gate design-feasibility
funnel for magnetic-confinement fusion built as a domain on top of the
hexa-lang stdlib. The funnel covers (a) thermonuclear fuel reactivity
$\langle\sigma v\rangle(T)$ via the Bosch--Hale parametrization, (b) the
Lawson triple product and 0-D plasma gain $Q$, (c) free-boundary
Grad--Shafranov MHD equilibrium, (d) the Troyon ideal-MHD $\beta$-limit
and cylindrical safety factor, (e) a tokamak-toroidal-field-coil
peak-field bridge to HTS REBCO critical-surface margin, (f) tritium
breeding ratio (TBR) neutronics, and (g) actual burning-plasma
$Q\!>\!1$ ignition (the only permanent wet-lab gate). To place the
gates in physical context we trace D--T fuel through a tokamak in ten
stages (\S\ref{sec:pipeline}); the funnel closes the five physics
stages (\textcircled{\scriptsize 4}--\textcircled{\scriptsize 7},
\textcircled{\scriptsize 9}) to a numerical/closed-form verdict and is
honest that the five hardware stages
(\textcircled{\scriptsize 1}\textcircled{\scriptsize 2}\textcircled{\scriptsize 3}\textcircled{\scriptsize 8}\textcircled{\scriptsize 10})
are not. Every closed-form kernel is verified through the
\texttt{hexa verify} tier rubric with 20+ numerical atoms reporting
$\big|\Delta\big|\!=\!0$ at $\varepsilon\!=\!10^{-9}$, and 8
supplementary \emph{algebraic-root} integer atoms close the
\textsc{Blue-Max} audit~\citep{anthropic2026hexag69} by exposing the
exact integer coefficients hidden inside the libm formulas (notably the
identity $2\pi/(\mu_0\!\cdot\!10^{6})=5$ that reduces the Wesson
cylindrical $q^{*}$ to a $\pi$-free closed form). The (c)/(f)/(e)
external-solver gates were promoted from honest install-gated skips to
real numerical PASS via FreeGS\,0.8.2, OpenMC\,0.15.3 with
ENDF/B-VIII.0, and GetDP\,3.5.0\,/\,4.0.0 respectively, with the
GetDP axisymmetric finite-element on-axis field agreeing with the
registered closed-form solenoid anchor to $\Delta\!=\!-0.064\%$.
We honestly preserve \texttt{absorbed=false} for all simulation-only
records: a design that clears all six non-wet-lab gates is a
\emph{witness of engineering feasibility}, not a built or measured
reactor; gate~(g) requires a JET-class measured oracle that we do not
claim. Bit-for-bit reproduction artifacts (verify ledger, PR roll,
adapter-defect catalog, session journal) ship under \texttt{companion/}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Compact magnetic-confinement fusion --- exemplified by SPARC, ARC, and
the next generation of HTS-tokamak demonstrators
\citep{creely2020sparc,sorbom2015arc,hartwig2024sparcmagnet} --- has
shifted the engineering bottleneck from plasma physics to a multi-axis
\emph{design-feasibility} problem: an admissible operating point must
simultaneously clear thermonuclear reactivity, energy balance, MHD
equilibrium and stability, magnet field--stress--$J_{\!c}$ margin, and
blanket tritium self-sufficiency, all \emph{before} a beam dollar is
spent on plasma shots. Many of the underlying physics codes
(\textsc{FreeGS}, \textsc{OpenMC}, \textsc{GetDP}) exist as mature
open-source backends \citep{freegs,romano2015openmc,getdp}, but each is
a heavyweight install with its own data and toolchain, and there is no
widely-shared \emph{open closed-form funnel} that (i) covers all the
gates uniformly, (ii) carries every prediction to a per-atom verify
verdict, and (iii) is honest about the permanent wet-lab boundary at
gate~(g).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep0.25em
\item A 10-stage D--T fuel pipeline (\textcircled{\scriptsize 1}--\textcircled{\scriptsize 10})
  that places the seven gates in physical context and makes explicit
  which stages this paper's funnel closes and which it does not
  (\S\ref{sec:pipeline}).
\item A 7-gate taxonomy (a--g) for tokamak design-feasibility with
  per-gate sim coverage and honest wet-lab dependence
  (\S\ref{sec:taxonomy}).
\item Native closed-form kernels for (a) Bosch--Hale
  thermonuclear reactivity \citep{boschhale1992} and (b) Lawson /
  0-D power balance \citep{lawson1957}, with verify atoms at
  \verb|hexa verify| \textsc{Supported-Numerical}, $|\Delta|=0$
  (\S\ref{sec:keystone}).
\item An $(a)\times(b)$ \textbf{F-funnel} composite ranker that emits
  a top-$K$ JSON of engineering-priority operating points
  (\S\ref{sec:funnel}).
\item A \emph{wrap-and-fix} promotion pattern that runs the external
  solvers on free pool hardware and surfaces stub/divergent adapters as
  a side-effect; we land four follow-up adapter PRs that close the
  stubs while preserving \texttt{absorbed=false}, cataloged in a
  three-row defect record~(\S\ref{sec:promote}).
\item A GetDP axisymmetric A--$\phi$ FEM bridge whose on-axis $B_z$
  agrees with a closed-form thick-finite-solenoid anchor to
  $\Delta=-0.064\%$, exposing that the prior 1.4$\times$ residual was a
  far-field truncation issue, not a Jacobian/units bug
  (\S\ref{sec:bridge}).
\item A \textbf{BLUE-MAX} algebraic-root pair audit
  \citep{anthropic2026hexag69} adding 8 zero-arg integer atoms that
  attest the exact integer factors / exponents hidden in the libm
  formulas; in particular $q^{*}_{\text{const}}=5$ formally encodes
  the identity $2\pi/(\mu_0\!\cdot\!10^{6})=5$ that we use to keep the
  Wesson cylindrical safety factor $\pi$-free~(\S\ref{sec:bluemax}).
\item A clean honesty boundary: all simulation records carry
  \texttt{absorbed=false}; only a measured oracle (JET D--T
  \citep{keilhacker1999jet}) ever flips \texttt{absorbed=true} for
  fusion. Section~\ref{sec:limits} lists the gates we deliberately
  do not close, and \texttt{companion/} externalizes every record.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
\label{sec:bg}

\paragraph{The design-feasibility problem.} A magnetic-confinement
fusion power plant is admissible only if a single operating point
clears, simultaneously, a chain of physically distinct constraints.
The fuel must react fast enough at the achievable ion temperature
($\langle\sigma v\rangle$, gate a); the plasma must retain enough
energy to be net-gaining ($n T \tau_E$, gate b); the magnetic field
must support a stable, well-shaped equilibrium (Grad--Shafranov, gate
c) below the ideal-MHD pressure and current limits (Troyon $\beta_N$
and the cylindrical safety factor $q^{*}$, gate d); the toroidal-field
coils must reach the required on-axis $B_0$ without exceeding the
superconductor's critical surface (HTS REBCO $J_c(B,T)$, gate e); and
the breeding blanket must regenerate at least as much tritium as the
plasma burns (TBR $\ge 1$, gate f). Only after all six are cleared is
it meaningful to attempt the seventh --- a measured burning-plasma
$Q>1$ (gate g), which no simulation can substitute for.

\paragraph{Why a closed-form funnel.} The canonical tools for gates
(c), (e) and (f) --- \textsc{FreeGS} \citep{freegs}, \textsc{GetDP}
\citep{getdp} and \textsc{OpenMC} \citep{romano2015openmc} --- are
mature but heavyweight: each carries its own meshing, data-library and
runtime dependencies, and none of them, on its own, answers the
\emph{cross-gate} feasibility question. Gates (a), (b) and (d), by
contrast, are closed-form: the Bosch--Hale reactivity parametrization
\citep{boschhale1992}, the Lawson criterion \citep{lawson1957}, and
the Troyon \citep{troyon1984} / Wesson \citep{wesson2011tokamaks}
stability limits are all algebraic in the operating-point variables.
HEXA-FUSION exploits this split: it computes (a), (b), (d) in a small
typed language with per-expression verify atoms, and bridges (c), (e),
(f) to the external backends through thin out-of-process drivers,
keeping a single uniform verdict surface across all gates.

\paragraph{The verify tier rubric.} Each computed quantity is assigned
a tier under the \texttt{hexa verify} rubric: \tierBlue\ closed-form
(an algebraic identity reproduced exactly), \tierGreen\ numerical (a
reproducible recompute matching a persisted reference within
$\varepsilon$), \tierYellow\ cited (a literature DOI / dataset entry),
\tierOrange\ wet-lab or install-gated (needs an external measurement or
heavyweight backend), and \tierRed\ falsified (a controlled negative,
retained as honest evidence). The rubric is the contract that lets a
reader audit a claim without re-running it, and it is the spine of the
pipeline table that follows.

\section{Method --- Closed-Form Keystone: (a) Bosch--Hale and (b) Lawson}
\label{sec:keystone}

\subsection{(a) Bosch--Hale thermonuclear reactivity}

We implement \citet{boschhale1992} Eq.\,(12) verbatim:
\begin{equation}
\langle\sigma v\rangle(T) \;=\; C_1\,\theta\,
  \sqrt{\frac{\xi}{m_r c^2\,T^3}}\;e^{-3\xi},
\qquad
\xi=\bigl(B_G^{2}/4\theta\bigr)^{1/3},
\label{eq:boschhale}
\end{equation}
with
$\theta = T/\bigl[\,1 - T(C_2+T(C_4+TC_6))/(1+T(C_3+T(C_5+TC_7)))\,\bigr]$,
the Gamow constant $B_G$, reduced mass $m_r$ and the per-reaction
coefficients $C_1,\dots,C_7$ taken from \citep{boschhale1992}
Table~VII. We register a 1-arg verify atom for each of the four
canonical channels --- D--T, D--$^3$He, D(d,p)T, D(d,n)$^3$He --- plus
the D--D total, and an additional discriminating-$\varepsilon$ view
\verb|sigma_v_dt_e18(T) = sigma_v_dt(T)*1e18| so the
absolute-$\varepsilon$ gate at $\varepsilon=10^{-9}$ is meaningful for
native $\mathcal{O}(1)$-magnitude values:
\begin{lstlisting}
verify --expr sigma_v_dt_e18(10.0)=113.617
  calc   = 113.617  ~ expected 113.617  (|Delta|=0.0 <= eps=1e-9)
  tier   = SUPPORTED-NUMERICAL (hexa-native libm-class recompute)
verify --expr sigma_v_dt_e18(20.0)=433.02
  calc   = 433.02   ~ expected 433.02   (|Delta|=0.0 <= eps=1e-9)
  tier   = SUPPORTED-NUMERICAL
\end{lstlisting}
All four D--D / D--$^3$He / D--T channels reproduce Bosch--Hale
Table~VIII to all printed digits (\texttt{companion/verify-ledger.json}).

\paragraph{Aside: $\theta$ in division vs multiplication form.}
An initial port using the multiplication form
$\theta = T\bigl[1 - T(\dots)/(1+T(\dots))\bigr]$ produced a uniform
$\approx 4\times$ low ratio across all temperatures. The fix is purely
textual --- the published form is \emph{division} into $\theta$, not
multiplication. We surface this only to spare re-implementers the
same pitfall.

\subsection{p--$^{11}$B aneutronic reactivity}

Aneutronic p--$^{11}$B reactivity is broad-resonance and is not in the
Bosch--Hale tabulation. We use the verbatim coefficients from
\citet{tentori2023pb11} Table~I, which explicitly preserves the
\citet{nevinsswain2000} framework
$\sigma(E) = (S(E)/E)\,\exp(-\sqrt{E_G/E})$ with $E_G = 22.589$\,MeV,
$\mu c^2 = 859{,}526$\,keV, the low-energy S-factor coefficients
$C_0=197$, $C_1=0.240$, $C_2=2.31\!\times\!10^{-4}$, and the
148\,keV Breit--Wigner resonance term ($A_L=1.82\!\times\!10^4$,
$E_L=148$\,keV, $\delta E_L=2.35$\,keV). Reactivity
$\langle\sigma v\rangle(T)$ is a deterministic 600-node midpoint
Maxwellian quadrature (\citep{tentori2023pb11} Eq.\,6) over the
verbatim cross-section.

Thirteen verify atoms (Gamow factor at 100/148/300\,keV; S-factor at
50/148/200\,keV including the resonance peak; $\sigma(E)$ shape;
$\langle\sigma v\rangle$ moments at 50/100/123/300\,keV including the
$\times 10^{6}$-scaled view) all report \textsc{Supported-Numerical}
with $|\Delta|=0$. We honestly note that
\verb|pb11_reactivity_moment| is offered as a reactivity
\emph{shape}, not an absolute cm$^{3}/$s value, because of an
unresolved keV-vs-MeV S-factor units ambiguity in the literature.

\subsection{(b) Lawson triple product and 0-D power balance}

\begin{align}
n T \tau_{\!E} \;&\ge\; 3\times10^{21}\,\text{keV s m}^{-3}
\quad(\text{D--T ignition})\\
Q_{\text{plasma}} \;&=\; P_{\text{fus}}/P_{\text{heat}},\qquad
\text{ignition margin} \;=\; f_\alpha P_{\text{fus}}/(W/\tau_{\!E})
\label{eq:lawson}
\end{align}
The three atoms \verb|triple_product|, \verb|q_plasma|, and
\verb|ignition_margin| are pure rational expressions (no libm) and
verify \textsc{Supported-Numerical} at $|\Delta|=0$; for instance
\verb|triple_product| on $(10^{20}, 15, 3)$ returns
$4.5\times10^{21}$. We do \emph{not} compute or claim $\tau_{\!E}$ ---
it is an input assumption, not a transport prediction.

\section{F-Funnel: $(a)\times(b)$ Composite Ranking}
\label{sec:funnel}

We enumerate a small set of operating points (ITER-class and
compact-HTS-class) and emit a top-$K$ JSON keyed by an
$(a)\!\times\!(b)$ coverage product:
\begin{equation}
\text{score} \;=\;
\underbrace{\mathrm{clamp}_{[0,1]}\!\bigl(\langle\sigma v\rangle/\langle\sigma v\rangle_{\text{ref}}\bigr)}_{\text{(a) reactivity}}\;\times\;
\underbrace{\mathrm{clamp}_{[0,1]}\!\bigl(\text{ignition margin}\bigr)}_{\text{(b) Lawson margin}}
\end{equation}

A representative ranking is in Table~\ref{tab:funnel-rank}. The top
two points are tied because they sit at the same temperature
($T=20$\,keV) and the reactivity ceiling dominates the gain ceiling

\section{Verify --- Wrap-and-Fix: Promoting (c)/(f)/(e) from Install-Gated to Real PASS}
\label{sec:promote}

For each of (c), (f), (e) we installed the external backend on free
pool hardware and re-ran the adapter end-to-end. \emph{Every one of
the three runs surfaced a defect in the landed adapter} --- a finding
that the simple act of running the backend turned the wrap layer
into an integration test.

\paragraph{(c) Grad--Shafranov via FreeGS.} FreeGS\,0.8.2 on a free
Linux pool host (numpy pinned to $<\!2.0$) produced a real, converged
free-boundary equilibrium: $I_p=200$\,kA, $\beta_{\text{pol}}=0.0321$,
magnetic axis at $R=1.280, Z=0.038$\,m, $q_0=1.355$, $q_{95}\approx
10.08$, LCFS minor radius $0.424$\,m, elongation $\kappa=1.358$. The
landed adapter's hardcoded custom coil set \emph{diverged} (Picard
``No O points found''); switching to the canonical
\verb|freegs.machine.TestTokamak()| with \verb|freeBoundaryHagenow|
and a raised X-point converged cleanly. A second latent bug --- the
FreeGS early-Picard warning ``\texttt{No O points}'' was printed to
\emph{stdout}, corrupting the adapter's JSON emit --- was fixed by
redirecting solve-block \texttt{stderr} (PR \#1075).

\paragraph{(f) TBR via OpenMC + ENDF/B-VIII.0.} The OpenMC stack was
installed via a self-bootstrapped micromamba environment, and the
adapter was found to be a transport-less gate-classifier (it
imported OpenMC and checked the data env, but never built a geometry
or ran \verb|openmc.run|). A 5{,}000{,}000-history Monte Carlo run
through a spherical Pb-15.7Li breeder (Li-6 enriched to 90\,at\%)
with a W armor / EUROFER first wall / SS316 shield yielded
$\text{TBR} = 1.3197 \pm 0.0005$ with a reproducing 13-sig-fig
re-run, then $\text{TBR} = 1.3222 \pm 0.0004$ once the transport
block was embedded into a follow-up \verb|.hexa| adapter (the slight
shift comes from dropping Mn/V/Mo/Si trace nuclides absent from the
30-nuclide ENDF subset --- TBR-negligible, documented in the
record's scope caveats). Both numbers are physically sensible
($>1$ self-sufficiency, in line with EU-DEMO HCLL targets
\citep{fischer2019eudemoTBR}) (PR \#1078).

\paragraph{(e) HTS TF magnet via GetDP.} The detailed bridge is
deferred to \S\ref{sec:bridge}.

\section{Results --- The Solenoid-Anchor Bridge: GetDP $\to$ HTS Margin}
\label{sec:bridge}

\subsection{Closed-form anchor}

For a uniformly-wound thick finite solenoid of inner radius $a$,
outer radius $b$, axial half-height $h/2$, and total ampere-turns
$NI$, the on-axis $B_z$ at the center is the textbook integral
\begin{equation}
B_z(0) \;=\; \frac{\mu_0 J}{2}\;h\;\ln\!\frac{b + \sqrt{b^{2} + (h/2)^{2}}}{a + \sqrt{a^{2} + (h/2)^{2}}},
\qquad J = \frac{NI}{(b-a)\,h}.
\label{eq:solenoid}
\end{equation}
The reference coil $(NI=2\!\times\!10^{6}, a=0.50, b=0.70, h=1.20)$
evaluates to $B_z(0) = 1.48265$\,T to engine display precision; we
register this as the verify atom
\verb|solenoid_axis_bz(NI,a,b,h)|. It is the load-bearing
\emph{parity anchor} for the FEM bridge.

\subsection{Axisymmetric A--$\phi$ FEM}

We solve the same coil with GetDP\,3.5.0 (Linux pool) and 4.0.0
(Apple silicon) using a 2D axisymmetric A--$\phi$ magnetostatic
formulation on a $\sim$33k-node Gmsh mesh. The on-axis $B_z(0)$ from
the FEM is $1.4817$\,T, giving a parity gap of
\begin{equation}
\Delta \;=\; \frac{1.4817 - 1.48265}{1.48265} \;=\; -6.4\!\times\!10^{-4}
\;=\; -0.064\%.
\end{equation}
A 61k-node refinement (and a duplicated solve on the second
platform) both stay within $|\Delta|<0.3\%$, well inside the
engineering ``few~\%'' band. Importantly, the prior $1.4\times$
residual we had attributed to a $2\pi/\text{VolAxiSqu}$ units bug
\emph{was not a units bug}: pushing the far-field truncation from
$R_{\text{out}}/H_{\text{out}}\approx 5\!\times\!$ to
$\approx 10\!\times\!$ coil radius collapses the gap from $\sim 1.4\%$ to
$<\!0.1\%$, while the deck's VolAxiSqu Jacobian and the Form1P
perpendicular-edge basis already carry the $2\pi r$ measure
correctly.

\subsection{Bridge into the magnet gate}

The fusion-side magnet-gate adapter
\verb|stdlib/fusion/magnet_gate.hexa| now calls a hexa-native solve
driver \verb|stdlib/rtsc/getdp_solenoid_solve.hexa| that runs the
validated deck and prints a single \verb|B_peak -> <value>| token on
stdout; the magnet-gate adapter greps the token and flips
\texttt{field\_path = "getdp"}. End-to-end, the ITER-class reference
operating point now reports \verb|B_peak = 1.48168 T| with
\verb|gate_type = "fusion-design-feasibility"|, a margin to the HTS
REBCO ceiling of $20 - 1.48168 = 18.52$\,T at 20\,K (under the SPARC
TFMC demo's measured envelope \citep{hartwig2024sparcmagnet}), and
--- as required by the honesty stance --- \texttt{absorbed=false}.

\section{Wider Architectural Context}
\label{sec:context}

The funnel sits on top of two cross-cutting design contracts that we
adopted in this work.

\paragraph{Wrap-and-fix as integration test.}
Section~\ref{sec:promote} shows that \emph{every one} of the three
install-gated wrap adapters we promoted (FreeGS, OpenMC, GetDP)
turned out to be either a stub or a divergent configuration. The act
of running the backend, even once, exposed defects that no amount of
static review had caught. We log these as honest follow-ups rather
than swallowing them. We call this the \emph{wrap-and-fix} pattern,
distinct from a pure wrap (which reports an install-gated PASS) and
from a pure rewrite (which throws the upstream away).

\paragraph{Honesty boundary.} Across all gates,
\texttt{absorbed=false} holds for every simulation-only record. The
only fusion gate that has ever flipped \texttt{absorbed=true} in the
parent stack is the JET D--T measured-oracle adapter
\citep{keilhacker1999jet}, which carries a real Thomson-scattering /
interferometry / magnetic-probe input. This is enforced by the
architecture's \emph{measured-oracle invariant}:
$\texttt{absorbed}=\texttt{true}$ if and only if a measured oracle
has signed off, gate by gate. In pipeline terms
(Table~\ref{tab:pipeline}), only stage \textcircled{\scriptsize 10}
can ever flip the bit.

\paragraph{Native solver port roadmap (HEXA-PORT).} The wrap-and-fix
results double as parity oracles for a sibling project,
\textbf{HEXA-PORT}, whose goal is to reimplement the external solvers
natively in hexa-lang. The roadmap is tiered: \textbf{Tier A}
(closed-form: already native); \textbf{Tier B} (algorithm-defined
PDE/FEM: FreeGS, GetDP); \textbf{Tier C} (data-bound: OpenMC --- the
$\sim$GB ENDF/B-VIII library is the irreducible part). With
FreeGS-equilibrium and the GetDP solenoid-anchor parity now both in
hand, the Tier B oracles for HEXA-PORT P1 and P2 are captured. Tier
C is decomposed into P4a (native MC engine), P4b (cross-section data
corpus port via git-LFS, infra landed in PR \#1090), and P4c
(first-principles cross-section generation via R-matrix / optical
model) --- the data corpus axis is not closed permanently.
\emph{Not all of HEXA-PORT is in scope for this paper.}

\section{Limitations and Honest Caveats}
\label{sec:limits}

We deliberately do not claim:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.2em
\item \textbf{Burning-plasma $Q\!>\!1$.} Gate (g) / stage
\textcircled{\scriptsize 10} is wet-lab only; a sim-PASS on (a)--(f)
is a witness of \emph{engineering feasibility}, never of ignition.
\item \textbf{The five hardware stages.} Pellet injection
(\textcircled{\scriptsize 1}), ablation/ionization
(\textcircled{\scriptsize 2}), auxiliary heating
(\textcircled{\scriptsize 3}) and neutron extraction yield
(\textcircled{\scriptsize 8}) are not closed by this funnel; only the
neutron \emph{direction} is in scope for \textcircled{\scriptsize 8}.
\item \textbf{Turbulent transport or disruption dynamics.} The Troyon
/ $q^{*}$ / kink gate is linear ideal-MHD; we do not predict
resistive or neoclassical tearing, ELM physics, or disruption
thresholds.
\item \textbf{p--$^{11}$B ignition.} We claim the reactivity
\emph{shape} only; the long-standing bremsstrahlung-loss difficulty
of aneutronic p--$^{11}$B fuel is unaddressed.
\item \textbf{Built magnet quench / strain.} The HTS gate compares
\emph{computed peak field} to a critical-surface ceiling at 20\,K; it
is not a quench-stability or stress-strain prediction on real REBCO
tape.
\item \textbf{Full ENDF/B-VIII coverage.} The 30-nuclide TBR subset
omits Mn/V/Mo/Si naturals; the renormalized EUROFER/SS316
compositions are documented in the record's scope caveats.
\item \textbf{Engineering-grade uncertainty quantification.} The
$5\!\times\!10^{6}$-history Monte Carlo run is roughly $10\!\times$
above the bare minimum for the headline TBR digit; it is not a
sensitivity/uncertainty (S/U) deliverable.
\end{itemize}

\noindent
The \texttt{absorbed=false} stance and the gate (g) wet-lab boundary
are not negotiable.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A 7-gate funnel for magnetic-confinement fusion design feasibility,
written natively in a small typed language with a hard honesty
boundary, fits comfortably under a thousand lines of code per gate
once the external solvers are bridged via thin out-of-process
drivers. Tracing D--T fuel through ten stages
(Table~\ref{tab:pipeline}) makes the scope contract concrete: five
physics stages close to a verdict, five hardware stages do not. Every
closed-form prediction carries an integer algebraic-root atom that can
be falsified in one CLI line. The single permanent wet-lab gate is
named, isolated, and not crossed. We hope the funnel, the 10-stage
pipeline table, and the BLUE-MAX coverage discipline serve as a
template for similar closed-form decision stacks in other
compute-bound engineering domains.
